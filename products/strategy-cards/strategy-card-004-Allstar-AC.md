## The Pain Point
HVAC in Houston is a seasonal war. May through September, every family-owned shop gets flooded with calls — AC is out, attic temperature is 140°, kids are sleeping at Grandma's. The shop that answers first gets the job. Every call that hits voicemail during a 95-degree Houston afternoon is a $3,000–$8,000 install going to whoever picks up.

Family operations have a hard ceiling: when all the technicians are on jobs (which is all day in summer), there's nobody left to answer phones. The owner is also likely running jobs. The phone becomes a liability.

**Estimated miss rate:** 30–45% during summer peak hours. This is where the real money leaks.

---

## What We'd Build for Allstar AC
An AI phone agent that:
1. **Handles the summer surge** without adding headcount
2. **Qualifies HVAC calls fast** — AC out vs. maintenance vs. new install vs. commercial — routes the right jobs to the right tech
3. **Books maintenance and non-emergency appointments** automatically
4. **Texts the owner** immediately for "no AC, house is 90 degrees, kids home" situations
5. **Captures every caller during peak hours** — the exact window when they can't answer

## The Math to Use on the Call
"In a Houston summer, what's the average install job worth to you? $4,000? $6,000? If you miss 2 installs in July because nobody answered the phone, that's $8,000–$12,000 gone. We're $199 a month. You'd have to miss one partial install for the math not to work."
