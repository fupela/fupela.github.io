---

## The Pain Point
Thirty-five years in East Houston. Kenneth's entire business is relationships — customers who called him in 1991 still call him today, and they pass his number to their kids. That referral network is *his* competitive moat. When a long-time customer calls for an emergency and hits voicemail, it's not just a lost job — it's a loyalty dent that takes years to build and one missed call to crack.

Emergency plumbing is the highest-margin, most time-sensitive category. Kenneth likely takes a lot of after-hours calls. At 35 years in, he probably wants to work fewer nights — but can't afford to let those calls go unanswered.

**Estimated miss rate:** 20–30% during job hours. Higher evenings/weekends as he scales back.

---

## What We'd Build for Kenneth
An AI phone agent that:
1. **Answers with warmth** — sounds like a professional front desk for "Kenneth's Plumbing," not a robot
2. **Handles the after-hours load** — emergency triage and booking while Kenneth rests
3. **Captures every caller** — even customers who "just wanted to check something quick" become booked appointments
4. **Texts Kenneth the job details** so he can decide whether to take it or schedule for morning
5. **Protects his referral relationships** — every caller gets an answer, a name, and a confirmation

---

## The Math to Use on the Call
"You've got 35 years of referrals — that's your business. One missed call to a loyal customer costs you the job AND potentially the relationship. If AI answers every call for $199 a month, it pays for itself the moment it catches one emergency you would have missed at 10 PM."

---

## Call Angles
- **Opener:** "With 35 years in East Houston, your customers trust you — they expect you to answer. What happens when you're on a job and that loyal customer from 2005 calls?"
- **Emotional hook:** "This isn't about getting more leads. It's about not letting down the customers you've already earned."
- **Practical hook:** "You've probably been thinking about slowing down. AI answering means you don't have to be on-call 24/7 anymore — the phone is covered."
- **Demo close:** Call the demo. Let him hear it handle "I've been a customer for years, there's water under my sink."

---

## Setup Notes
- Yahoo email + 35 years = likely not a tech early adopter → lead with simplicity: "Your phone number stays the same, nothing changes for your customers"
- Long tenure means established pricing and service scope → easier for AI to learn his service menu
- Retirement horizon angle: AI buys Kenneth back evenings and weekends without losing revenue
- Key objection to prep for: "I like talking to my customers myself" → "You still will — for the jobs you book. This just makes sure none of them fall through while you're busy."
