## The Pain Point
Solo licensed master plumber. When Eric is under a sink, in a crawl space, or driving between jobs — the phone rings and nobody answers. Emergency plumbing calls have a 5-minute window before the customer dials the next number. A missed call from someone with a burst pipe is a $500–$2,000 job handed to a competitor, not a voicemail.

**Estimated miss rate:** 30–40% of calls during work hours. Higher on weekends.

---

## What We'd Build for Eric
An AI phone agent that:
1. **Answers every call within 2 rings** — even when Eric is on a job
2. **Qualifies the emergency** — burst pipe, clogged drain, no hot water, etc.
3. **Books non-emergency jobs** directly to Eric's calendar
4. **Texts Eric immediately** for anything urgent (active leak, gas smell, flooding)
5. **Captures name + address + problem** for every single call — nothing falls through

The AI answers as "LTP Plumbing" — sounds professional, not robotic. Eric's brand stays intact.

---

## The Math to Use on the Call
"How many calls do you miss in a week? If it's 5, and the average plumbing job is $400, that's $2,000 a week — $100,000 a year — going to whoever answered first. We charge $199 a month. That's the math."
