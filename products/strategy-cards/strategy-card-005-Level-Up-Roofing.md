## The Pain Point
Roofing in Houston has a specific surge pattern: storm season. When a hailstorm or derecho rolls through the Houston metro, Rafael's phone goes from quiet to 200 calls in 48 hours. Fast-growing means he's already operating near capacity during normal times — he can't hire a call center for storm season and lay them off in December. The calls come in waves, and he misses the tail end of every wave because he's already running jobs.

Storm-damage insurance jobs are high-value ($8,000–$20,000+ per roof) and time-sensitive — customers are calling 5 roofers simultaneously. First callback wins.

**Estimated miss rate:** 15–25% normal season. 40–60%+ during storm surges. The surges are where the real money is.

---

## What We'd Build for Rafael
An AI phone agent that:
1. **Handles storm surge volume** — no headcount ceiling, answers call 1 and call 200 with equal speed
2. **Qualifies storm damage vs. general roofing** — insurance job vs. repair vs. new build
3. **Books on-site inspection appointments** directly to Rafael's calendar
4. **Captures insurance company + claim number** during the intake — saves Rafael 5 minutes per call
5. **Texts Rafael** for any job that mentions "active leak" or "structural damage" for immediate follow-up

---

## The Math to Use on the Call
"After the last big storm, how many calls did you miss? If you missed 10 and your average storm job is $12,000, that's $120,000 in calls you couldn't get to. We're $499 setup and $199 a month. The ROI conversation is over in 30 seconds."
