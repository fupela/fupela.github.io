## Discovery Call Script (15 minutes)

### Opening (2 min)

"Hey [Name], this is John with Ship Clean with Pella. Thanks for taking the time. I know you're busy so I'll keep this quick — I work with [plumbers/HVAC/electricians] in Houston and I help them stop missing calls. Before I tell you anything, I want to understand how your business handles calls right now. Can I ask you a few questions?"

### Section 1: Current Call Handling (3 min)

**"How many calls does your business get in a typical week?"**
→ Write down the number. This is their total call volume.

**"How many of those do you actually answer yourself?"**
→ Subtract from total. That's their miss rate. If they say "most of them" — probe deeper.

**"What happens to the ones you miss?"**
- Voicemail? → "How many actually leave a message?" (usually less than half)
- Call them back? → "How long does it take you to call back?" (if more than 5 min, they've already called someone else)
- Nothing? → "That's money walking out the door."

**"What time of day do you miss the most calls?"**
→ This tells us when to set up warm transfers vs. message-taking.

### Section 2: The Business (5 min)

**"What are your top 3-5 services — the ones that bring in the most revenue?"**
→ These become the AI's qualification focus. We don't need to know every service, just the money-makers.

**"What's an average job worth to you?"**
→ This is the key number. If they say $300 and they miss 8 calls/week, that's $2,400/week in lost revenue.

**"Do you take emergency calls? After hours?"**
→ If yes, this is a major selling point — AI handles after-hours automatically.

**"Do you charge a service call fee or diagnostic fee?"**
→ We need to know this so the AI can mention it (or not) on calls.

**"What services do you NOT do that people call about?"**
→ This prevents the AI from booking jobs they can't handle.

### Section 3: The Pain (3 min)

**"When you miss a call, what do you think happens to that customer?"**
→ Let them answer. They usually say "they call someone else." Reinforce: "You're right — 80% of people call the next business within 5 minutes."

**"If you could capture even half of those missed calls, what would that mean for your business?"**
→ Let them say the number. It's always more than our $199/mo fee.

**"Have you ever tried an answering service or virtual receptionist before?"**
→ If yes: "What didn't you like about it?" (usually: too expensive, didn't know their business, sounded robotic)
→ If no: "Most of our clients hadn't either. They thought it would be complicated or expensive. It's neither."

### Section 4: The Close (2 min)

**"Here's what I'd do for you — and I can show you right now if you want."**

→ DEMO TIME: Call their number (or your demo number) and let the AI answer. Let them hear it. This is the close.

**"It answers every call, 24/7. Qualifies the lead, books appointments directly to your calendar, texts the customer a confirmation. If it's an emergency, it transfers straight to your phone. You get a dashboard showing every call, every lead, every appointment. Monthly report showing exactly how much revenue it's capturing for you."**

**"$499 to set it up, $199 a month. If it captures one extra job a week, it pays for itself."**

**"Want to get started, or do you have questions?"**

## Strategy Generator

### After the call, I generate:

1. **Client Profile Card** — name, business type, services, call volume, miss rate, average job value
2. **Recommended Call Flow** — custom to their business:
   - Greeting script
   - Qualification questions (specific to their services)
   - When to warm transfer vs. take a message
   - Emergency handling rules
   - Appointment booking flow
3. **Bland AI Configuration** — I build the agent based on their answers
4. **Estimated ROI** — "Missing X calls/week × $Y avg job = $Z in recovered revenue"
5. **30-60-90 Day Plan** — what happens after they go live

### Strategy Templates by Business Type:

#### Plumber
- **Greeting:** "Thanks for calling [Business Name], this is [AI name]. How can I help you today?"
- **Must-collect:** Type of plumbing issue, address, is it an emergency (burst pipe, sewage backup = emergency)
- **Warm transfer trigger:** Emergency calls, water heater installations, commercial jobs
- **Emergency handling:** Transfer to owner's cell, if no answer → text owner immediately + text customer "We got your emergency request, [Owner] will call you within 15 minutes."
- **Don't book:** Gas line work, remodel jobs (refer these — liability)

#### HVAC
- **Must-collect:** AC or heating, unit type (residential/commercial), is it out completely or partial, address
- **Warm transfer trigger:** No AC in summer (emergency), commercial units, new installations
- **Seasonal note:** "Summer is our busy season, we can typically get to you within [X] days"
- **Don't book:** Commercial HVAC, ductwork redesign

#### Electrician
- **Must-collect:** Type of electrical issue, is there active danger (sparking, burning smell = emergency), address
- **Warm transfer trigger:** Active danger (transfer immediately), power out, panel upgrades
- **Don't book:** New construction (too complex for phone qualification)

#### Roofer
- **Must-collect:** Type of issue (leak, storm damage, full replacement), address, how long has the issue been present
- **Warm transfer trigger:** Active leaks, storm damage (time-sensitive for insurance), commercial roofs
- **Don't book:** Solar panel installation

#### Landscaper
- **Must-collect:** Type of service (maintenance, design, install), property size (rough estimate), address
- **Warm transfer trigger:** Commercial properties, large design/build projects
- **Seasonal note:** "Spring and summer are our peak — we recommend scheduling regular maintenance now"

#### Pest Control
- **Must-collect:** Type of pest, severity (one bug or infestation), address, is it residential or commercial
- **Warm transfer trigger:** Infestations, commercial properties, termite signs
- **Don't book:** Wildlife removal (specialized, different license)
