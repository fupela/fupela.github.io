**Hook:**
I spent a year answering phones at a plumbing company in Houston.

**Body:**

When I started, I thought plumbing was about pipes, wrenches, and showing up on time.

I was wrong.

Plumbing is about **phones.**

Specifically: who answers them, how fast, and what happens next.

**The Phone Is the Business**

Our owner was a master plumber. 30 years of experience. Could diagnose a slab leak by listening to water pressure.

But his business was bleeding money, and he didn't know why.

I pulled the call logs. We were missing 35-40% of incoming calls.

Not during busy periods. Not after hours. **All day long.**

Why? Because when you're the owner AND the lead plumber AND the dispatcher, the phone is enemy #1.

**The Customer Journey**

Here's what actually happens when someone's pipe bursts:

1. They panic
2. They Google "plumber near me"
3. They call the first 3 numbers
4. The first person who answers calmly and says "I can have someone there today" gets the job
5. The other two companies? They never get a callback

No one compares Yelp reviews during a flood. No one checks BBB ratings. They just need someone to **pick up**.

**What "Missed Call" Really Means**

I tracked it for a month:

- 10 calls/day average
- 3.5 missed per day
- Average job value: $1,200
- Close rate on answered calls: 45%

That's $1,890 in lost revenue per day.

Per week: $13,230
Per month: $52,920

From not answering the phone.

**The Voicemail Trap**

Our voicemail said: "Thanks for calling [Company]. Leave your name, number, and a brief message, and we'll call you back as soon as possible."

Here's what actually happened:

- 60% of callers hung up without leaving a message

**A 10% callback success rate.**

"I'm under a house."

"I'm on a roof."
"I can't hear it over the sawzall."
"My hands are covered in sewage."

These aren't excuses. They're **physics.**

You can't answer a phone while soldering a pipe. You shouldn't answer a phone while operating a snake machine. You physically can't hear a phone while running a jackhammer for slab leak repair.

**The After-Hours Problem**

Plumbing emergencies don't happen 9-5.

- Water heaters fail at 10 PM
- Pipes burst at 6 AM

Our voicemail after hours: "Leave a message and we'll call you back Monday."

Homeowners hearing that on Saturday night? They call the next company.

I watched our owner "mark" missed calls to return later.

He'd have 8-12 "call back" entries in his phone by noon.

He'd return 3-4. The rest?

"I got busy."
"I forgot."
"By the time I called, they already hired someone."
