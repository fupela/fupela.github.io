## The Spring Rush Is Real — and Brutal

April through June in Houston is landscaping's Black Friday. Homeowners who ignored their lawns all winter suddenly want:

- Weekly mowing ($200–$400/month)
- Spring cleanup and mulching ($800–$1,500)
- Full landscape redesign ($5,000–$20,000)
- Irrigation repair before summer heat ($300–$800)

**The problem:** Your crews are in the field by 7 AM. Your office? Unmanned by 10 AM. The phone rings. Nobody answers. The homeowner calls the next company on Google.

## The Math That Hurts

A typical Houston landscaping company gets 8–12 calls per day during spring rush. Here's what happens:

- **30% go to voicemail** (you're on a job site, equipment running, can't hear the phone)
- **10% hang up after 4 rings** (Google gave them 10 other options)
- **Of the 60% you capture, half are price shoppers** (not your ideal customer)

**Net result:** You're fighting for 30% of leads that actually matter — and losing most of them to whoever picks up first.

Landscaping isn't like retail where customers browse and buy later. It's **urgency-driven**:

1. **"My sprinkler is gushing water"** — needs same-day service
2. **"We're hosting a party Saturday"** — needs cleanup by Friday
3. **"The HOA sent a violation notice"** — needs immediate fix

These callers don't leave voicemails. They call the next company. And the next. Until someone answers.

## Why Crews Can't Answer the Phone

It's not about laziness or bad habits. It's physics:

- **Equipment noise:** Mowers, blowers, edgers — you can't hear a phone
- **Dirty hands:** Mud, grass clippings, fertilizer — you don't want to touch your phone
- **Focus:** Operating machinery safely requires attention
- **Professionalism:** Customers don't want to hear "WHAT? I CAN'T HEAR YOU" over a leaf blower

Your best people are your worst phone answerers. By design.
