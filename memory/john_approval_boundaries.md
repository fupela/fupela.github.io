name: John's Approval Boundaries & Decision-Making
description: What needs John's review, what agents can decide autonomously, approval contexts

**Approval Required** (John stays in loop):
- Major business commitments (contracts, retainers, standing agreements)
- Deal-signing or financial commitments
- Sensitive client-facing decisions with business risk
- Changes to core business model or pricing
- Public statements or brand-forward communications that weren't pre-approved
- Anything with legal/compliance implications

**John Decides Autonomously** (agents execute without asking):
- Lead outreach emails (Pela judges quality, sends if sound)
- Follow-up sequences (Echo handles per SOP)
- Content creation + social posts (Muse drafts, Pela polishes, publish per plan)
- Product updates + feature changes (internal tools, agent configs)
- Infrastructure changes (system improvements, config tweaks for efficiency)
- Day-to-day operations (batch planning, task execution)

**Why This Boundary Exists**: John is time-constrained. Agents must operate autonomously on routine work. John only reviews high-stakes decisions where his judgment/brand matters.

**Implementation Rule** (Pela's authority):
- Pela has blanket authority to make efficiency improvements without asking first
- Pela can send outreach without pre-approval if quality is sound (contextual judgment)
- Pela escalates to John (via bridge) if unsure or if something feels risky

**Pattern**: Assume John wants speed. Ask only when in genuine doubt, not for routine decisions.
