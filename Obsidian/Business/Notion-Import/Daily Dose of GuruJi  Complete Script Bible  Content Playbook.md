## 5-Minute Podcast Episode 1
"Why You're Tired, Broke, and Still Checking Their Instagram"
### Chunk 1 — Intro + Why Am I Always Tired? (~60 sec)
Welcome to Daily Dose of Guruji, where ancient wisdom meets modern nonsense. I am your Guruji, and today we are answering the questions you are too afraid to ask your therapist but not too afraid to type into Google at three in the morning. Let us begin.
Our first question comes from a young man who asks, Guruji, why am I always tired? I sleep eight hours but I wake up exhausted. What is wrong with me? My friend, nothing is wrong with your body. Something is wrong with your life. You are tired because you are living a life that drains you and calling it responsibility. You wake up, immediately grab your phone, scroll through bad news for twenty minutes, drag yourself to a job that makes your soul feel like a wet sock, come home, eat something out of a box, watch three hours of a show you do not even enjoy, and then wonder why your body is filing for bankruptcy. That is not a sleep problem. That is a life problem. Your body is not a machine. It is a messenger. And right now it is sending you a very clear message. It is saying, I am not tired of sleeping. I am tired of this. Real rest is not just closing your eyes. Real rest is living a life you do not need to constantly escape from.
### Chunk 2 — Why Can't I Save Money? (~60 sec)
Our second question. Guruji, I make decent money but I can never save anything. Where does it all go? I will tell you where it goes. It goes to the version of yourself you are performing for strangers. You bought shoes you did not need to impress people you do not like. You ordered food delivery seventeen times this month because cooking felt like too much effort, but then you spent forty-five minutes choosing a filter for your coffee photo. You have subscriptions you forgot about. You are paying for three streaming services and you watch the same show on all of them. My friend, the problem is not your income. The problem is your identity. You have tied your self-worth to your spending. You think looking successful is the same as being successful. It is not. Wealth is not what you spend. Wealth is what you keep. And more importantly, wealth is needing less. The richest person in the room is not the one with the most things. It is the one who needs the fewest things to be happy. So cancel the subscription you forgot about. Cook your own food. And stop buying things to fill a hole that was never shaped like a product.
### Chunk 3 — Why Do I Keep Attracting Toxic People? (~60 sec)
Next question. Guruji, why do I keep attracting toxic people? The same type of person keeps showing up in my life. My friend, I am going to tell you something you will not enjoy hearing. You are not attracting toxic people. You are accepting them. There is a very big difference. A toxic person does not break into your life like a burglar. You open the door, invite them in, make them chai, give them the comfortable seat, and then six months later wonder why your house is on fire. The question is not why do they keep coming. The question is why do you keep letting them stay. And I will tell you why. Because somewhere inside you, you believe that is what you deserve. Someone taught you that love is supposed to be difficult. That if it does not hurt, it is not real. That is a lie. Love is not supposed to feel like a hostage negotiation. The truth is, you will only attract what you believe you are worth. If you believe you deserve chaos, chaos will find your address every single time. So raise your standards. Not your walls. Walls keep everyone out. Standards let the right ones in.
### Chunk 4 — The Fisherman Story (~60 sec)
Now it is time for Guruji's final thought. A businessman was standing on the beach in a small village watching a fisherman bring in his catch. He said, that is a nice fish. How long did it take you to catch it? The fisherman said, not long. The businessman said, why don't you stay out longer and catch more fish? The fisherman said, this is enough for my family today. The businessman said, but if you caught more, you could sell them, buy a bigger boat, hire people, build a company, and in fifteen or twenty years you could sell the business and retire. The fisherman said, and then what would I do? The businessman said, then you could move to a small village by the beach, relax, and go fishing whenever you want. The fisherman smiled and said, what do you think I am doing right now? We spend our entire lives chasing a future that looks exactly like the present we are ignoring.
### Chunk 5 — Closing (~60 sec)
And that is your daily dose for today. Actually, that was more like a weekly dose. Consider it a bulk purchase. Let me leave you with this. You do not need more information. You have read the books. You have watched the videos. You have saved three hundred motivational posts that you have never looked at again. What you need is not more knowledge. What you need is to actually use what you already know. You know you should put your phone down. You know you should stop spending money you do not have. You know you should stop giving your time to people who drain you. You know all of this. The gap between where you are and where you want to be is not information. It is action. So pick one thing from today. Just one. And do it. Not tomorrow. Not next Monday. Not after your next birthday. Today. Because tomorrow is the favorite word of people who never change. I am your Guruji, this has been Daily Dose of Guruji, and I will see you in the next episode. Until then, be kind to yourself. But not so kind that you stop growing. There is a difference. Namaste.
