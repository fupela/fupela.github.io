JediFlow Comprehensive Business Plan
Prepared for John
Focus: scalable lead capture, follow-up automation, and local business growth services

1. Executive Summary
JediFlow should start as a productized service that helps local businesses capture more leads, follow up automatically, book more appointments, and reduce manual administrative work. The initial wedge is outbound and lead follow-up automation. Once results are proven, JediFlow can expand into website upgrades, traffic generation, CRM setup, booking flows, review automation, and eventually software.

Core offer:
Help local businesses automate lead capture and follow-up so they convert more opportunities without hiring more office staff.

2. Best Starting Market
Start with one narrow niche in one city. Houston is a strong launch market.

Recommended first niche:
- Home service businesses in Houston, such as HVAC, roofing, plumbing, electrical, remodeling, and landscaping

Why this niche works:
- Lead response speed directly affects revenue
- Owners understand ROI quickly
- Many still rely on manual admin workflows
- Website, follow-up, and booking problems are easy to diagnose

3. Customer Pain Points
- Missed leads from slow or inconsistent follow-up
- Weak or outdated websites
- No clear CRM or lead pipeline
- Too much manual admin work
- Poor visibility into booked jobs and campaign performance
- Too much dependency on staff for repetitive tasks

4. Core Offer Structure
Entry Offer:
- Lead follow-up automation setup
- Email and optionally SMS sequences
- Lead routing and reply handling rules
- Basic monthly performance reporting

Monthly Retainer:
- Campaign monitoring
- Copy and prompt optimization
- Sequence improvements
- Reply handling logic updates
- Monthly KPI review

Upsells:
- Website rebuild or landing page creation
- Lead capture forms and CRM integration
- Missed-call text back and appointment booking
- Review request automation
- Traffic generation or ads management
- Workflow automation for admin tasks

5. Product Vision
The long-term scalable product is a local business growth and automation engine.

Core platform modules:
- Lead sourcing by niche and city
- Business audit and opportunity scoring
- CRM or unified lead inbox
- AI-assisted personalization for outreach
- Automated follow-up sequences
- Appointment booking and reminders
- Dashboard for sends, replies, and booked calls

6. MVP Recommendation
Do not build a giant platform first. Build the smallest reliable engine that can generate results.

MVP features:
- Import leads from CSV or sheet
- Optional local business discovery workflow
- Lead qualification and tagging
- Prompt-based personalized email generation
- Daily scheduled sending with batch controls
- Reply detection and stop-on-response rules
- Simple reporting dashboard
- Manual approval mode at launch

7. Recommended Go-to-Market Strategy
- Sell the service before overbuilding software
- Pick one niche and one pain point
- Use outbound to land the first 3 to 5 customers
- Document every repeated step as an SOP
- Turn the proven workflow into a productized service
- Only then build software around proven demand

8. Pricing Ladder
Suggested pricing model:
- Setup fee: $500 to $2,500 depending on complexity
- Monthly management: $300 to $1,500+
- Website or landing page upsell: $500 to $3,000+
- Advanced automation upsell: custom pricing

Pricing should track clear business value, not just tool access.

9. 30-Day Execution Plan
Week 1:
- Choose the first niche and offer
- Define the exact promise and pricing
- Create a one-page landing page and short pitch

Week 2:
- Build the MVP workflow for lead import, email generation, and sending
- Create two to three outreach sequences
- Set up KPI tracking

Week 3:
- Start targeted outreach in Houston
- Run discovery calls
- Refine messaging based on replies

Week 4:
- Close first customers
- Deliver manually with light automation
- Document workflow and identify upsells

10. 90-Day Roadmap
- Month 1: narrow niche, validate offer, close first clients
- Month 2: improve automation quality, create SOPs, refine pricing
- Month 3: standardize onboarding, reporting, and upsells

11. Operational Safeguards
- Protect email deliverability with low daily caps at first
- Use separate sending domains if volume increases
- Honor unsubscribe and reply-stop rules
- Track every send, reply, and booked call
- Avoid vague AI positioning, focus on revenue and time savings

12. What Success Looks Like
- First 3 paying customers in a single niche
- Repeatable onboarding and delivery process
- Clear monthly retainer revenue
- Two to three profitable upsells
- Enough proof to justify software development

13. Final Recommendation
Build JediFlow as a lead capture and follow-up automation business first. Use the service to generate cash, proof, and customer insight. Then convert the best-performing pieces into a scalable software platform.
