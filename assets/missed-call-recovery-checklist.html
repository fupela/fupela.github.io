The problem

A missed call is often a missed job.

For home-service businesses, customers usually need help now. If nobody answers and no one follows up quickly, they often call the next company on Google.

The goal is not to sell perfectly. The goal is to respond before the lead goes cold.

Rule: every new lead should get a useful first response within 60 seconds whenever possible.

Checklist

Capture this before the lead goes cold.

  • ☐ Customer name
  • ☐ Phone number
  • ☐ Service needed
  • ☐ Urgency level
  • ☐ Address or service area
  • ☐ Preferred appointment time
  • ☐ Emergency or non-emergency
  • ☐ Source of the lead, if known
  • ☐ Who was alerted
  • ☐ Follow-up status
Workflow

The missed-call recovery workflow

  1. Detect the missed call, voicemail, form, or inbound message.
  2. Send an immediate reply.
  3. Ask what service they need.
  4. Confirm urgency.
  5. Collect address or service area.
  6. Alert the owner or dispatcher.
  7. Log the lead.
  8. Follow up again if they do not respond.

Start manually. Automate once the workflow is clear.

Scripts

Use these short replies.

Missed call text

Hi, this is [Business Name]. Sorry we missed your call — what service do you need help with today?

After-hours reply

Thanks for reaching out to [Business Name]. We’re currently after hours, but we received your request. If this is urgent, reply EMERGENCY.

Emergency triage

Got it. What’s the service address, and can you briefly describe what’s happening?

Follow-up

Just checking back — do you still need help with [service]? We can help schedule the next available appointment.

DIY setup

Start simple before you automate.

  • Use one main business phone number.
  • Turn on missed-call and voicemail alerts.
  • Keep a Google Sheet of leads.
  • Create saved reply templates.
  • Review missed calls twice per day.
  • Track how many leads were recovered.
