Amex Business Gold: ChatGPT and Squarespace Credits for AI-First Businesses

The useful card benefits are not just travel points. For a small business building with AI, credits for ChatGPT, domains, shipping, office supplies, and business meals can map directly to the operating stack.

John Jedlowski · 5 min read · May 18, 2026

I usually do not get excited about business credit cards unless the credits line up with tools a business is already going to use. The American Express Business Gold Card is interesting right now because several of its benefits fit the way AI-first small businesses are starting to operate.

The short version: if you already spend money on ChatGPT, domains, websites, shipping, office supplies, or business meals, the Amex Business Gold credits are worth checking. Do not chase benefits you will not use. But if these are already real expenses, the card can support a practical AI business stack.

The credits that matter for an AI-first business

The card currently has a few credits that are especially relevant for builders, creators, consultants, agencies, and small business operators:

  • Up to $300 per year for eligible ChatGPT Business purchases made directly with OpenAI in the U.S., after enrollment.
  • Up to $150 per year for eligible Squarespace purchases, after enrollment.
  • Up to $240 per year in flexible business credits, structured as up to $20 per month at eligible merchants such as FedEx, Grubhub, and U.S. office supply stores.

The annual fee is $375, so this only makes sense if the credits match expenses you would have anyway. The pro-AI part is that the card now supports a stack many modern businesses are already building around: AI tools, web presence, domains, shipping, and everyday operations.

Why the ChatGPT credit is the headline

ChatGPT has moved from “interesting tool” to normal business infrastructure for a lot of people. It helps with:

  • drafting emails and follow-ups
  • writing landing pages and offer copy
  • summarizing calls and customer notes
  • building SOPs and checklists
  • researching markets and competitors
  • turning rough ideas into publishable content

If ChatGPT is already part of your business workflow, a statement credit tied to eligible ChatGPT Business purchases is not a gimmick. It is a credit against a real operator tool.

Important detail: check the current Amex and OpenAI terms before assuming your exact plan qualifies. Enrollment is required, and offers can change.

The Squarespace credit can be useful even if you do not build on Squarespace

The Squarespace credit is more useful than it first sounds because Squarespace also sells domains.

That means you can register a domain through Squarespace and point it to a website hosted somewhere else, such as Cloudflare Pages, Vercel, GitHub Pages, or another platform. You do not have to move your whole website to Squarespace just because the domain is registered there.

For an AI-first business, that can support:

  • a campaign domain
  • a lead magnet domain
  • a niche service landing page
  • a productized offer
  • a simple redirect to an existing website
  • a brand test before building a full site

The key is to buy domains with a job. A domain should point to an offer, proof asset, landing page, demo, or newsletter capture. Random domains still become clutter, even if a credit offsets the purchase.

The monthly flexible credit is operational, not flashy

The flexible business credit is structured monthly. That means it rewards discipline, not one-time enthusiasm.

If you regularly use eligible merchants for shipping, office supplies, or occasional business meals, it can help offset normal business spend. If you forget to use it, it is not worth counting in your mental math.

My read

This is not a card I would get just because benefits sound good. It is a card I would consider if the credits match tools and services I already plan to use.

For AI-first small businesses, the stack is becoming clearer: AI subscription, web/domain infrastructure, content, customer communication, and simple operational systems. The Amex Business Gold Card is interesting because some of its credits now line up with that stack.
