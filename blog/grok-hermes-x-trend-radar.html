AI Agents + X Research

Using a Grok Subscription Inside Hermes Agent as an X Trend Radar

The useful version of “AI for social” is not asking for random viral tweets. It is giving an agent live context from X, a clear audience, and a repeatable loop for turning trends into useful posts.

John Jedlowski · 7 min read · May 18, 2026

xAI now lets Grok subscribers connect their account directly to Hermes Agent. For Jedaiflow, the interesting part is not novelty. It is that one OAuth login can make Grok available inside the same persistent agent system that already handles research, memory, drafts, and scheduled workflows.

The short version: Grok inside Hermes can become a practical X trend radar. Use it to scan active conversations, extract what people are actually reacting to, draft a useful angle, and feed that back into Jedaiflow content without chasing every trending hashtag.

What changed

xAI says Grok subscribers can now use their Grok account and subscription inside Hermes Agent, Nous Research's open-source personal agent. Hermes runs persistently, connects to messaging tools, and keeps long-term memory across sessions.

The Hermes docs describe the provider as xAI Grok OAuth. It uses a browser-based OAuth login with an existing SuperGrok subscription, stores and refreshes the session automatically, and does not require a separate XAI_API_KEY for this provider.

That matters because the workflow becomes simple: use the subscription you already pay for, connect it once, and call Grok from Hermes when the task needs X-native context, fast research, or trend-aware drafting.

Why this matters for X

X is not just a place to publish. It is a live market of language. People show you which phrases are spreading, which objections keep coming up, what operators are frustrated by, and which examples create replies.

Most creators use AI backward here. They ask for “10 tweets about AI automation” and get generic content. A better loop is:

  1. Scan the live conversation around a narrow topic.
  2. Identify the posts that are getting real replies, not just passive likes.
  3. Extract the angle, language, and tension behind the response.
  4. Draft one useful Jedaiflow take that adds something concrete.
  5. Turn the best take into a website article, X post, or reply thread.

The four signals worth watching

1. Reply density

Likes can be lightweight. Replies are heavier. If a topic is producing questions, disagreement, examples, or “how do I do this?” comments, it is usually a better target for a Jedaiflow post.

2. Repeated phrasing

Watch for the words people naturally repeat: “agents,” “workflow,” “missed calls,” “operator,” “prompt stack,” “local business,” “automation fatigue.” Repeated phrasing tells you how the market names the problem.

3. First-hour momentum

If posts in a niche get replies quickly, the conversation is alive. That does not mean copy the post. It means publish while the audience is still paying attention.

4. Practical confusion

The best content opportunities often come from confusion. If people are excited about a tool but unclear on setup, cost, limits, or use cases, that is an article waiting to be written.

What not to optimize for

Do not turn Jedaiflow into a trend-chasing account. Chasing every viral phrase creates short-term reach and long-term confusion. The better filter is: Does this trend help a builder, creator, or small business operator make a clearer decision?

If the answer is no, skip it. If the answer is yes, use Grok and Hermes to turn it into a clear artifact: a short thread, a practical checklist, a field note, or a resource page.

A simple Grok + Hermes X research loop

  1. Pick three lanes. For Jedaiflow: AI agents, practical workflow automation, and home-service lead response.
  2. Run a short scan. Ask Hermes with Grok to summarize what changed today on X in those lanes.
  3. Extract the tension. What are people excited about, confused by, or arguing over?
  4. Choose one useful angle. Do not post five generic takes. Pick one helpful point of view.
  5. Draft one root post and three replies. The root earns attention. The replies teach.
  6. Turn durable ideas into website posts. If the idea will still be useful next week, write it on the site and use X to distribute it.

Example prompt

Prompt: “Use Grok to scan X for active discussion around AI agents, Hermes Agent, Grok subscriptions, and small business automation. Return: 5 repeated phrases, 3 points of confusion, 3 high-reply angles, and one Jedaiflow post idea that teaches instead of hypes.”

My read

The real advantage is not that Grok can write tweets. The advantage is that Grok has better native context for X, while Hermes has memory, scheduling, tools, and the ability to turn research into actual files and workflows.

Put together, that gives Jedaiflow a stronger rhythm: learn what the market is saying, write something useful, publish it on owned media, then use X for distribution and conversation.

What I would automate first

I would not start with auto-posting. I would start with a daily research brief:

  • Top 5 X conversations in Jedaiflow's lanes.
  • Repeated phrases and objections.
  • One recommended reply opportunity.
  • One recommended original post.
  • One article idea only if the topic has durable value.

That keeps the system useful without letting automation flood the account with low-quality posts.
