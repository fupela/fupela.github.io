Why Pest Control Companies Need AI Phone Answering

John Jedlowski · 8 min read · April 2026

I spent a year answering phones at a plumbing shop in Houston. The owner also ran pest control. Same problem in both businesses: the phone rings, nobody’s there, and the customer calls the next number. No voicemail has ever booked a job.

Pest Control Is Peak-Season Business

March through September in Houston is pest season. Termite swarms, mosquito pressure, rats in attics after every rain. Calls spike. Techs are in crawlspaces and backyards. Nobody’s answering the phone.

And it’s worse than plumbing. A customer with termites is under contract — they call you first. But a residential lead with their first roach sighting? They call everyone. The company that answers books the inspection. Everyone else is invisible.

The Real Cost of a Missed Pest Call

Average pest control lead in Houston: $280 initial treatment + $45/month recurring. Average customer lifetime: 18 months. That’s $1,090 per customer.

One missed emergency call at 9 PM on a Tuesday — a homeowner who just found a snake in their garage — is a $1,000+ customer who books with whoever answers.

What an AI Phone Agent Actually Does

  • Picks up every call in your company name — instantly, any time of day
  • Qualifies the job — type of pest, urgency, property size, first-time or recurring
  • Books the inspection — on your real calendar, with customer details
  • Texts you the summary — hot lead flagged for immediate callback, everything else scheduled

A part-time phone answering service handling after-hours calls runs $1,200–$1,800/month. They take breaks, get sick, and still miss calls. An AI phone agent is $199/month flat — covers every call, every night, never calls in sick.
