# Cold Email Templates for AI Agency Outreach
### 5 Proven Templates — Customize and Send

---

## Template 1: The Website Audit

**Best for:** Businesses with an outdated or broken website

**Subject:** Quick thought about [Business Name]'s website

```
Hi [First Name],

I was looking for a [business type] in [city] and came across 
[Business Name]. Love what you're doing — especially [specific 
detail: "your custom cake designs" / "your 5-star reviews"].

I noticed your website might be missing a few things that could 
help bring in more customers:

- [Specific issue: "It's not showing up well on mobile" / 
  "There's no way to book online" / "The contact form seems broken"]

I help local businesses get a modern, professional website up 
in 48 hours. Would it be worth a quick 5-minute chat to see 
if I could help?

No pressure either way — just thought it was worth mentioning.

[Your Name]
[Your Website]
```

## Template 2: The No-Website Opener

**Best for:** Businesses found on Google Maps/Yelp with no website

**Subject:** [First Name] — had an idea for [Business Name]

```
Hi [First Name],

I found [Business Name] on Google Maps and it looks like you're 
doing great work — [X] reviews averaging [X] stars is solid.

I noticed you don't have a website yet, which means you might be 
missing out on customers who search online before visiting.

I build professional websites for local businesses — mobile-friendly, 
with online booking and contact forms built in. Most are done in 
under 48 hours.

Would you be open to seeing a quick mockup of what [Business Name] 
could look like online? Free, no commitment.

[Your Name]
```

## Template 3: The Value-First Follow-Up

**Best for:** Following up on a previous email (send on day 3)

**Subject:** Re: [Previous Subject Line]

```
Hi [First Name],

Just floating this back up — I know you're busy running 
[Business Name].

I actually went ahead and sketched out a few ideas for how 
your online presence could look. Happy to share if you're 
curious.

If the timing isn't right, no worries at all. Just say the 
word and I won't follow up again.

[Your Name]
```

## Template 4: The Referral Ask

**Best for:** Happy clients or warm contacts

**Subject:** Quick favor?

```
Hi [First Name],

Hope [Business Name] is going well! I've really enjoyed 
working with you on [project you did for them].

Quick question — do you know any other business owners 
who might need help with their website or online marketing? 
I'm looking to take on 2-3 more clients this month.

If anyone comes to mind, I'd really appreciate the intro. 
And of course, if you ever need anything on your end, I'm 
here.

Thanks!
[Your Name]
```

## Template 5: The Social Proof Pitch

**Best for:** Once you have 1-2 completed projects

**Subject:** How [Similar Business] got 3x more calls from their website

```
Hi [First Name],

I recently helped [Client Name/Type — e.g., "a barbershop in 
Houston"] rebuild their website. In the first month they saw:

- [Result: "40% more calls from Google"]
- [Result: "Online booking filled 15 slots in the first week"]
- [Result: "Showed up on the first page for '[service] near me'"]

I'm looking to do the same for 2-3 more [business type] 
businesses this month. Would [Business Name] be a good fit?

Happy to show you exactly what I did — takes 5 minutes.

[Your Name]
[Link to case study or portfolio]
```

## Email Rules That Matter

1. **Personalize the first line.** Generic = deleted. Mention their business name, a recent review, their specialty.
2. **Keep it under 100 words.** Business owners are busy. Respect that.
3. **One call to action.** Don't ask them to visit your site AND book a call AND reply. Pick one.
4. **Follow up.** 80% of sales happen after the 5th contact. Most people stop after 1 email.
5. **Send Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11 AM local time.** Highest open rates.
6. **Track everything.** Use a spreadsheet: who you emailed, when, did they reply, follow-up date.

## Follow-Up Schedule

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Day 0 | Send initial email |
| Day 3 | Follow-up #1 (Template 3) |
| Day 7 | Follow-up #2 (new angle or value add) |
| Day 14 | Final follow-up ("closing the loop") |

After 4 touches with no response, move on. Don't burn bridges — they might come back later.
