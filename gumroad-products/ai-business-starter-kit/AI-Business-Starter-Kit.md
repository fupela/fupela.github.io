## Part 1: The Business Model

### What You're Actually Selling

Small businesses don't care about AI. They care about:
- Getting more customers
- Saving time on repetitive work
- Looking professional online
- Not hiring another employee

Your job is to solve these problems using AI tools. You're not selling "AI services" — you're selling outcomes.

### The Service Stack (Pick 1-2 to Start)

**Website Creation — $300-800 per site**
Most small businesses have either no website or a terrible one. You can build a professional site in 2-4 hours using AI-assisted tools. Include hosting setup, domain connection, contact forms, and mobile optimization.

Tools: Claude Code, Cursor, Bolt, v0 by Vercel, Framer
Your cost: $0-20/month in hosting
Time per project: 2-4 hours
Profit margin: 90%+

**Email Marketing Setup — $200-500 per setup + $99/month management**
Set up automated email campaigns that nurture leads and bring back past customers. Most small businesses have a customer list but do nothing with it.

Tools: Mailchimp, ConvertKit, AI for copywriting
Your cost: Free tier covers most small businesses
Time per project: 2-3 hours setup, 1 hour/month maintenance

**Social Media Content — $200-400/month per client**
Use AI to generate a month's worth of social media posts in 1-2 hours. Schedule them out automatically. Offer this as a monthly retainer.

Tools: Claude/ChatGPT for copy, Canva for graphics, Buffer/Hootsuite for scheduling
Your cost: ~$30/month in tools
Time per client: 2 hours/month

**Workflow Automation — $300-1,000 per project**
Identify repetitive tasks in a business (invoicing, appointment reminders, follow-ups) and automate them. This is the highest-value service because it saves hours every week.

Tools: Zapier, Make.com, n8n, custom scripts
Your cost: $20-50/month in tools
Time per project: 3-6 hours

### Pricing Strategy

Start lower than you think you should. Your first 3-5 clients are about proof, not profit.

| Stage | Pricing | Goal |
|-------|---------|------|
| First 3 clients | 30-50% below market | Get testimonials and case studies |
| Clients 4-10 | Market rate | Build sustainable revenue |
| 10+ clients | Premium pricing | You have proof, charge accordingly |

**Example starter pricing:**
- Basic website: $400
- Website + email setup: $600
- Monthly maintenance: $99/month
- Full package (site + email + social): $800 + $199/month

## Part 3: Finding Clients

### The 5 Channels That Actually Work

**Channel 1: Local Outreach (Fastest)**
Walk into local businesses. Look at their website on your phone before you walk in. If it's bad (or doesn't exist), you have your opening.

Script: "Hey, I noticed your website might be due for an update. I help local businesses get a modern site set up quickly — would you be open to seeing what that could look like for [business name]?"

Why it works: Face-to-face is hard to ignore. Most competitors are hiding behind emails.

**Channel 2: Cold Email (Scalable)**
Find businesses with bad/no websites. Send personalized emails.

Subject lines that work:
- "Quick question about [Business Name]'s website"
- "I had an idea for [Business Name]"
- "[Owner Name] — 2-minute question"

Email template:
```
Hi [Name],

I came across [Business Name] and noticed you might not have 
a website yet (or your current one could use a refresh).

I help local businesses get a professional website up and 
running in 48 hours — mobile-friendly, with online payments 
and a contact form built in.

Would it be worth a 5-minute call to see if this could help 
bring in more customers for [Business Name]?

Either way, love what you're doing with [specific compliment].

[Your Name]
```

Key rules:
- Personalize the first line (mention something specific about THEIR business)
- Keep it under 100 words
- One clear call to action
- Follow up on day 3 and day 7 if no response

**Channel 3: Facebook/Nextdoor Groups**
Join local business owner groups. Don't pitch immediately. Answer questions, be helpful, share knowledge. After a week of being present, post something like:

"I've been helping local businesses get set up with modern websites and automated email marketing. Happy to do a free audit of your online presence if anyone's interested — no strings attached."

**Channel 4: Referrals**
After your first client, ask: "Do you know any other business owners who might need something similar?" Offer a $50 referral bonus.

**Channel 5: Your Own Content**
Document your journey. Post on Twitter/X, Reddit, or YouTube. "Day 1 of building an AI business" content performs well. People follow the journey, and some become clients.

## Part 4: Delivering the Work

### Client Onboarding (First 24 Hours)

When someone says yes:

1. Send a simple proposal/invoice (use Stripe or PayPal)
2. Collect: business name, logo (if they have one), colors, what they want on the site
3. Set expectations: "You'll see a first draft in 24-48 hours"
4. Get to work

### Building a Website in 2-4 Hours

Here's the actual workflow:

1. **Pick a template/framework** (15 min) — Use Next.js, Framer, or a template that fits their business type
2. **Generate content with AI** (30 min) — Feed Claude the business details, have it write all the copy
3. **Build the pages** (60-90 min) — Home, About, Services, Contact. Use AI coding tools to move fast
4. **Set up forms & payments** (30 min) — Contact form, Stripe/Square integration if needed
5. **Mobile optimization & polish** (30 min) — Make sure it looks good on phones
6. **Deploy** (15 min) — Vercel, Netlify, or their hosting

### The Follow-Up (This Is Where Money Multiplies)

After delivering the website:
- Check in at 1 week: "How's the new site working out?"
- Check in at 1 month: "You've had X visitors this month. Want to talk about driving more traffic?"
- Offer the monthly maintenance plan: "$99/month — I handle updates, backups, and keep everything running"

Every one-time client is a potential recurring revenue client.

## Part 5: Scaling With Automation

### What to Automate First

1. **Lead finding** — Scripts that scan Google Maps, Yelp, or directories for businesses without websites
2. **Email sequences** — Automated follow-ups so you never forget to follow up
3. **Proposal generation** — Templates that auto-fill with client details
4. **Reporting** — Automated monthly reports for maintenance clients showing site traffic

### The Agent Framework

As you grow, think of your AI tools as team members:

- **Lead Hunter** — Finds and qualifies potential clients (runs daily)
- **Outreach Agent** — Sends and manages email campaigns
- **Builder Agent** — Assists with website and tool creation
- **Account Manager** — Monitors client sites, sends check-in reminders

You don't need to build all of these on day one. Start manual, identify what takes the most time, automate that first.

## Part 6: Numbers That Matter

### Break-Even Analysis

| Expense | Monthly Cost |

This is the key to sustainability:

- 20 clients on $99/month = $1,980/month that renews automatically
