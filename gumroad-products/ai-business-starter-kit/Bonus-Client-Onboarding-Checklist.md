# Client Onboarding Checklist
### Everything You Need From a New Client

---

## Step 1: Before They Pay

- [ ] Confirm scope of work (what exactly are you delivering?)
- [ ] Agree on price and payment terms
- [ ] Set timeline expectations ("You'll see a first draft in 48 hours")
- [ ] Send invoice/payment link (Stripe, PayPal, or Gumroad)
- [ ] Payment received? Move to Step 2.

---

## Step 2: Information Gathering

Send this to your client. Keep it simple — don't overwhelm them.

### Business Basics
- [ ] Business name (exactly as they want it displayed)
- [ ] Business type/industry
- [ ] Physical address (if applicable)
- [ ] Phone number
- [ ] Email address
- [ ] Business hours

### Branding
- [ ] Logo file (PNG or SVG preferred, any format accepted)
- [ ] Brand colors (ask "what colors do you like?" if they don't have brand guidelines)
- [ ] Any existing website URL (even if it's bad, useful for reference)
- [ ] Social media links (Facebook, Instagram, etc.)

### Content
- [ ] Brief description of their business (2-3 sentences)
- [ ] List of services/products they offer
- [ ] Pricing (if they want it displayed)
- [ ] 3-5 photos of their business, work, or products
- [ ] Any testimonials or reviews they want featured
- [ ] "About" info — how long in business, what makes them different

### Functionality
- [ ] Do they need online booking/scheduling?
- [ ] Do they need to accept payments online?
- [ ] Contact form — what info should it collect?
- [ ] Any specific pages they want? (Gallery, Menu, Team, FAQ, etc.)
- [ ] Do they have a domain name already? If so, what is it?

---

## Step 3: Building (Your Workflow)

- [ ] Set up project folder for this client
- [ ] Choose template/framework based on business type
- [ ] Generate website copy using AI (feed it the business details)
- [ ] Build homepage, about page, services page, contact page
- [ ] Add their photos, logo, and branding
- [ ] Set up contact form
- [ ] Set up payments/booking if needed
- [ ] Test on mobile
- [ ] Test on desktop
- [ ] Test all links and forms
- [ ] Deploy to staging URL for review

---

## Step 4: Client Review

- [ ] Send staging link to client: "Here's your first draft — let me know what you think!"
- [ ] Collect feedback (limit to 2 rounds of revisions)
- [ ] Make requested changes
- [ ] Get written approval: "Looks good, go live!"

---

## Step 5: Launch

- [ ] Connect their domain (or purchase one for them)
- [ ] Deploy to production
- [ ] Set up SSL certificate (HTTPS)
- [ ] Test everything one more time on live URL
- [ ] Set up Google Analytics (optional but recommended)
- [ ] Submit site to Google Search Console
- [ ] Send client the final live URL
- [ ] Celebrate with them: "Your new site is live!"

---

## Step 6: Post-Launch (Where Recurring Revenue Lives)

- [ ] Check in at 1 week: "How's the site working out?"
- [ ] Check in at 1 month: share basic traffic stats
- [ ] Offer maintenance plan: "$99/month — updates, backups, support"
- [ ] Offer add-on services: email marketing, social media content
- [ ] Ask for a testimonial
- [ ] Ask for referrals

---

## Quick Copy-Paste Messages

### Sending the intake form:
```
Hey [Name]! Excited to get started on your website. 
I just need a few things from you to get going — I've 
attached a short list. Don't stress about having everything 
perfect, just send what you have and I'll work with it!
```

### Sending the first draft:
```
Hey [Name]! Here's your first draft: [staging URL]

Take a look on both your phone and computer. Let me know:
1. What you love
2. What you'd change
3. Anything I'm missing

I've got 2 rounds of revisions included, so don't hold back!
```

### Announcing the launch:
```
[Name] — your new website is LIVE! 

[live URL]

Everything is set up and working. Here's what's included:
- Mobile-friendly design
- Contact form (you'll get emails at [their email])
- [Any other features]

I'll check in next week to see how things are going. 
Congrats on the upgrade!
```

### Pitching the maintenance plan:
```
Hey [Name], your site has been live for a month now and 
it's looking great! 

I wanted to mention — I offer a monthly maintenance plan 
for $99/month that covers:
- Unlimited small updates and changes
- Security monitoring and backups
- Monthly traffic reports
- Priority support

Most of my clients find it helpful so they don't have to 
worry about the tech side. Want me to set that up for you?
```
